# How to Create Your First Project

This guide walks you through creating your first project in TaskFlow, from
sign-up to your first task.

## Before you start

- A TaskFlow account (sign up at taskflow.example.com)
- 2–3 minutes

## Steps

### 1. Sign in and open the dashboard

Go to [taskflow.example.com](https://taskflow.example.com) and sign in.
You'll land on the dashboard, which shows all projects you're a member of.
If this is your first time, the dashboard will be empty.

### 2. Click "New project"

Click the blue **+ New project** button in the top-right corner of the
dashboard. A dialog will appear.

### 3. Fill in the project details

- **Name** — give your project a clear, specific name, like
  "Website Redesign" or "Q3 Marketing Plan". Avoid generic names like
  "Work" — they make it harder to find things later.
- **Description** (optional) — a short summary of what this project is for.
  This is visible to all members.
- **Color and icon** — pick a color and emoji icon. These help you visually
  tell projects apart in the sidebar.
- **Default view** — choose Kanban, List, Calendar, or Timeline. Kanban is
  the most common starting point.

### 4. Click "Create project"

You'll land on the project page, which is currently empty.

### 5. Add your first task

Click the **+ New task** button (or press `N` on your keyboard). Enter:

- **Title** — be specific. "Write homepage copy" is better than "Homepage".
- **Assignee** — if you're working solo, assign it to yourself
- **Due date** — optional, but very useful
- **Priority** — Low, Medium, High, or Urgent

Click **Save** to create the task.

### 6. Add more tasks

Keep adding tasks until your project reflects the work you actually need to
do. Most people start with 5–15 tasks. Don't worry about getting it perfect
— you can edit, delete, or reorder tasks anytime.

### 7. (Optional) Invite teammates

If you're not working alone, click the **Members** icon in the top-right of
the project and add teammates by email. See the "How to invite team
members" guide for details.

## Tips

- **Start small.** Create one project and fill it with real work before you
  try to use TaskFlow for everything.
- **Use labels early.** Labels like "bug", "design", or "blocked" make
  filtering much easier once you have lots of tasks.
- **Try all four views.** Switch between Kanban, List, Calendar, and
  Timeline in the view selector to see which feels right for your workflow.
- **Set up notifications.** Visit **Settings → Notifications** to control
  what you get emailed and when.
