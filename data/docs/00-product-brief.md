## What is TaskFlow?
TaskFlow is a web-based project management tool that helps teams plan,
organize, and track their work across projects, tasks, and deadlines.

## Target users
- Solo freelancers managing client work
- Small remote teams (5–20 people)
- Mid-sized companies (up to 200 people) running multiple projects in parallel

## Competitors
- Trello
- Asana
- Notion

## Core features
- **Projects** — top-level containers for related work
- **Tasks & subtasks** — assignable work items with due dates, priorities, and labels
- **Multiple views** — Kanban board, List, Calendar, and Timeline (Gantt)
- **Comments & @mentions** — threaded discussion on any task
- **File attachments** — up to 100 MB per file on paid plans
- **Integrations** — Slack, Google Drive, GitHub, Zapier
- **Mobile apps** — iOS and Android companion apps

## Platforms
- **Web app** — primary product, works in any modern browser
- **iOS app** — native, optimized for phone and iPad
- **Android app** — native
- **No desktop app** — the web app is the desktop experience

## Brand personality
**Friendly and approachable, but professional.**

- Uses plain English, avoids jargon
- Warm but never cutesy (no emojis in docs, no exclamation marks everywhere)
- Treats users as smart adults
- Tone reference: closer to Notion than to Mailchimp

## Things TaskFlow is NOT
To avoid scope confusion, TaskFlow explicitly does **not** include:
- AI-powered auto-planning or task assignment
- Time tracking / billing
- CRM features
- Code repository hosting

These may be roadmap items but are not part of the current product.
